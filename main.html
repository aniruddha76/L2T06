Gaming as a Hobby: Power Up Your Fun

I have been gaming for as long as I can remember, and it is a hobby that is shared by an estimated 3.1 billion people worldwide.
In this page I share why gaming is special to me and will always be.

What makes gaming so special...

I could answer simply that it is entertaining, games are entertainment, therefore I am entertained. but it is much deeper than that. There are two parts to this answer.
The first part is regarding single player games such as The Witcher 3. Games like these have massive, detailed and sprawling worlds that you can get completely lost in for hours at a time. Not only are the worlds incredible but so are the stories, characters, music, combat and many other things about it. These all work together to make a truly immersive and amazing experience that no other form of entertainment can. There are countless different single player games to experience and a truly massive variety of genres, from fantasy roleplaying games to horror to realistic sim racing.

The second part is regarding multiplayer games. There is nothing like playing a game with friends online or local co-op.
Multiplayer games allow you to not only have a great time with friends or family, but also meet some awesome new people too. I have met so many great people from all over the world that I not only play games with, but talk to regularly outside of gaming too. Gaming is a way to connect with people that you otherwise may have never even met in the first place, and to me that is something quite special.

Gaming is such a massively diverse hobby, so there really is something for everyone to enjoy.

My top picks

Here I share with you a selection of three games that hold a special place in my heart. They are immersive experiences that have deepened my love for gaming.
These games transport you to incredible worlds, challenge your skills, and inspire your imagination. These virtual realms that have brought countless hours of joy and excitement into my life...
and the many others who have experienced them.

1. Elden ring
Elden Ring is an open world action role-playing game developed by FromSoftware, who are masters at their craft and have created an entire genre of games. This game is the culmination of everything they have learnt from their previous titles and has seen success beyond belief. Elden Ring is everything I have ever wanted in an open world game. This is first and foremost due to its gameplay and world building. You can truly get lost in this massive diverse open world, and right from the start you are free to explore wherever you lay your eyes. The gameplay is punishing and challenging, so when you overcome a tough enemy it is a rewarding experience that not many other games can match. The art design is truly a marvel to behold and at times I found myself just walking slowly or riding my spectral steed staring at the glorious views. It is like you are exploring a painting. The lore and story is extremely well thought out too, and it is not delivered like it is in most games. In Elden Ring (as well as FromSoftware's precious titles) a bit part of the story is told through environmental storytelling and through item descriptions which you can read in-game. This gives a sense of mystery and urges the player to figure things out for themselves and leaving some things up for interpretation. To me this is the perfect game.

2. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt
The Witcher 3 is a massive open world adventure game that you can get lost in for hundreds of hours. In this game you take the role of Geralt of Rivia, a witcher, one of the last monster slayers for hire. The story will keep you wanting to know what happens next, and you also have the freedom of making choices that will shape your journey and the fate of others, for netter or for good, it is up to you to decide. The game features a vast and visually stunning open world that feels alive and filled to the brim with detail. You will explore diverse landscapes, from lush forests to war-torn villages, and even entirely different continents, each with its own unique atmosphere. There is also a card game in the game called Gwent, which you could get lost in for hours too, and going all over the regions in the game challenging other players and collecting cards to strengthen your deck. This game is packed with so many quests and things to do that you may never even do all there is to do, and each playthrough you will still find new things and make different choices resulting in an almost new experience.

3. Monster Hunter World
Monster Hunter is a very special series to me. It is difficult to choose which one is best, but Monster Hunter World would have to be it. In this game, though the story may be decent, it is all about hunting monsters, carving their materials once you slay them, and then crafting better weapons and armor back at your home base. Then you go out once again and hunt even tougher monsters, and craft even better gear. This is the core gameplay loop in Monster Hunter games, and before you know it you have a thousand or more hours in the game. You get a true feeling of being a monster hunter, having to prepare for hunts before departing on the hunt, such as crafting items like healing potions and monster traps, and eating at the canteen which gives you special bonus stats depending on what meal you pick on the menu. If you want a game with near unlimited replay value, Monster Hunter might be the game for you.
